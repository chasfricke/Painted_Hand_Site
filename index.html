About Me!



My professional career began working as an outdoor science teacher during the school year, and at adventure-based camps during summer breaks. I did this for 6 years, and couldn’t have been happier.

As time went on, I wanted to expand my skill set, so I took a position teaching kids to code at a small, but rapidly expanding edtech company called MV Code Club. It was a complete 180° from my previous work, but I quickly found that I loved the problem-solving and creativity required to build even the simplest software programs.

After getting their summer camps up and running, I took a job with Steve & Kates, a large day camp company with 39 locations. After word got out that I’d coded a new digital bingo game that replaced the old card-based version, I was approached by the owners and asked about rolling it out company-wide.

I went straight to work with their development team in downtown San Francisco to completely redesign the activity. After multiple iterations, I was able to deliver an end product that the kids loved, required fewer staff, and provided a more enjoyable pickup experience for our parents. This was my first introduction to working in an agile dev team environment, and I was hooked.

After 14 straight summers at camp, I was excited to take on a new challenge and pursue a career in coding. In late 2017, I entered the Galvanize Web Immersive Program, and after 6 months of intense study, I’m excited to pursue this next chapter in my life. I’d love to be able to help design and build products that are used in the edtech space, but am open to any opportunity that leverages technology to help the greater good.
